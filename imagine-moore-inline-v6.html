Message from the President

This year, Bishop Moore Catholic proudly celebrates its 70th anniversary, an incredible milestone reflecting a strong foundation built on faith, excellence, and service by our foundresses, the Sisters of St. Joseph of St. Augustine.

Over the past seven decades, much has changed, but the core values that define Bishop Moore remain steadfast. Today, we continue to thrive, welcoming a freshman class of more than 425 students. As the only Catholic high school in Orlando, we are truly blessed to provide a Catholic education to all who seek it.

Looking ahead, we are excited about the opportunities before us, particularly the chance to expand and create a dedicated space where our students can cultivate and express their God-given talents in the arts.

I invite you to learn more about the plans for the Center for the Arts at Bishop Moore Catholic. We welcome your guidance, feedback, and questions. Your participation and support for Imagine Moore will help our dancers, thespians, singers, artists, and musicians flourish.

Whole-child formation

Emphasis on the Arts

At Bishop Moore Catholic, we focus on developing the whole child. Recognizing the importance of the arts, every student is required to take at least one course in fine arts, performing arts, or a related discipline.

Teaching the arts to high school students promotes creativity, critical thinking, and emotional expression. It boosts problem-solving skills, collaboration, and confidence while enhancing cultural awareness. It can also improve performance in other subjects, helping students grow both personally and academically.

“My daughters have found their community in the performing arts. I wholeheartedly support building a dedicated performing arts space that will provide all of our arts students the resources and environment to truly thrive.”
Sheila Aland
Current Parent of Theatre Students & pARTners Founder

In 2024, we established the pARTners in the Arts program, enlisting founding members to contribute at various levels. Members enjoy benefits such as recognition and reserved seating while knowing their support directly enhances the arts at BMC. The program currently includes 25 founding members representing local businesses, current families, alumni, grandparents, future families, and partner schools.

Lack of Dedicated Space

Rising enrollment has placed significant pressure on our facilities. Our fine arts building currently includes just four shared classrooms, increasingly insufficient to meet growing demand and the graduation requirement that every student complete a performing or fine arts credit.

Performance Space

Our partnership with the Dr. Phillips Center gives students a world-class experience, but off-site productions present logistical challenges. A dedicated on-campus venue will allow us to expand and enhance performance offerings.

Presence on the Drive

The new building will create a prominent architectural showcase, distinguishing the campus and strengthening Bishop Moore Catholic’s visibility along the main road.

More Parking

The project will also provide dedicated parking for faculty and staff.

Room to Grow

As we move to the new Center for the Arts building, we will vacate academic spaces in Bishop Dorsey Hall. This new complex gives Bishop Moore Catholic an important opportunity to redesign those spaces for greater capacity and additional curricular offerings.

With our evolving curriculum and the growing needs of 21st-century learners, our Programs of Distinction, first launched with Aerospace, have expanded to include Biomedical, Business & Finance, Computer Science, and Engineering, with a Law Program on the horizon.

Future Law Program

The Law Program will give students a foundation in the American legal system. Throughout the program, students will study courtroom procedures, ethics, and key areas of law.

Law Program students may also participate in extracurricular legal opportunities, including:

  • Mock Trial Team
  • Moot Court Team
  • Speech and Debate
